Here is a thorough synthesis of the on-call setup based on that article:

Your on-call setup uses a paired on-call rotation strategy. This means that two team members share the on-call responsibilities simultaneously rather than having a single person on call alone. The key benefits of this approach include:

- Providing mutual support and backup during the on-call period.
- Improving incident response times by having two people available.
- Reducing the risk of single points of failure in incident management.
- Enhancing the quality and speed of incident resolution.

To make this paired on-call rotation effective, the following practices are important:

- Clear documentation of incidents, responsibilities, and procedures to ensure both on-call members are aligned.
- Well-defined handoff procedures between on-call pairs to maintain continuity.
- Regular debriefs after on-call shifts to identify issues and improve the process.
- Communication and coordination between the paired members to share workload and knowledge.

This setup is designed to improve operational stability and reduce downtime by ensuring that on-call duties are shared and supported.

Additionally, the paired on-call rotation interacts with other operational processes such as escalation procedures and approval authorities, which may be involved when incidents require higher-level decisions or interventions.

In summary, your on-call setup is a paired on-call rotation system that emphasizes collaboration, documentation, and backup to enhance incident management effectiveness and reliability.
