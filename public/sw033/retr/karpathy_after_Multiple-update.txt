The package managers used over time, from earliest to latest, based on the knowledge base, are:

1. Karnex Pack - Used for dependency management with best practices including keeping dependencies updated, cleaning unused packages, and documenting configurations. This was mentioned in the context of paired on-call rotation and release-train branching strategy discussions around 2023-03-05.

2. Dranith Stack - Another package management system with best practices such as semantic versioning, regular updates with testing, and locking dependencies to specific versions or ranges. Discussed around 2023-03-07.

3. Synthari Modules - The most recent package manager adopted by the team during a transition phase. It is used to streamline dependency management, requiring clear setup practices and communication. This transition was noted around 2023-03-10.

Thus, the chronological order is:
Karnex Pack → Dranith Stack → Synthari Modules

These details are drawn from the sessions on 2023-03-05, 2023-03-07, and 2023-03-10 respectively [[concepts/karnex-pack-package-management]], [[concepts/dranith-stack-package-management]], [[concepts/synthari-modules-package-management]].
